## Brackets & rates

**1.** Earned-income brackets for **2025**:

| Upper threshold (₪/year) | Marginal rate |
|---|---|
| 84,120 | 10% |
| 120,720 | 14% |
| 193,800 | 20% |
| 269,280 | 31% |
| 560,280 | 35% |
| above 560,280 | 47% |

Source: [Protocol — Income Tax Levels 2025](https://en.protocol.co.il/income-tax-rates/).

**2.** The **35%** bracket in **2024** runs **₪269,281 – ₪560,280** (annual). Because the tax brackets are frozen at 2024 values for 2025-2027 (see answer 4), the 2025 brackets above also reflect 2024 values. Sources: [Protocol — 2025 brackets](https://en.protocol.co.il/income-tax-rates/), [Goldfarb — 2025 Israeli Tax Changes](https://www.goldfarb.com/2025-israeli-tax-changes-worth-following/).

**3.** **₪560,281** in 2025 — the 47% marginal rate applies to every additional shekel above ₪560,280. Source: [Protocol](https://en.protocol.co.il/income-tax-rates/).

**4.** The freezing law is **חוק ההתייעלות הכלכלית (תיקוני חקיקה להשגת יעדי התקציב לשנת התקציב 2025), התשפ"ה-2024**, passed by the Knesset on **26 December 2024**. It freezes the indexation of amounts under §120B of the Income Tax Ordinance — **income-bracket ceilings, credit-point values, social discounts, recognized donation amounts** — and the §121B surtax (מס יסף) brackets, for tax years **2025–2027**. Sources: [Knesset announcement (16-Dec-2024)](https://main.knesset.gov.il/News/PressReleases/pages/press16.12.24p.aspx), [Efraty — תיקוני חקיקה 2025-2027](https://www.efraty.com/%D7%97%D7%95%D7%A7-%D7%94%D7%94%D7%AA%D7%99%D7%99%D7%A2%D7%9C%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%94%D7%9B%D7%9C%D7%9B%D7%9C%D7%99%D7%AA-%D7%AA%D7%99%D7%A7%D7%95%D7%A0%D7%99-%D7%97%D7%A7%D7%99%D7%A7%D7%94-%D7%9C%D7%94/), [Capitax bill text PDF](https://www.capitax.co.il/Attachments/04122024.pdf).

**5.** **14%.** Both 100,000 ₪ and 105,000 ₪ fall inside the **84,121 – 120,720** band. Source: [Protocol](https://en.protocol.co.il/income-tax-rates/).

## Credit points (נקודות זיכוי)

**6.** **₪2,904 per year** for tax year **2024**. Sources: [wintax — נקודות זיכוי 2025](https://wintax.co.il/service/income-tax-credit-points/), [Sagrir — נקודות זיכוי 2025](https://sagrir.com/%D7%A0%D7%A7%D7%95%D7%93%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%96%D7%99%D7%9B%D7%95%D7%99-%D7%9E%D7%9E%D7%A1-%D7%94%D7%9B%D7%A0%D7%A1%D7%94-%D7%9C%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA-2025-%D7%9B%D7%9C-%D7%9E%D7%94-%D7%A9%D7%A6/).

**7.** **₪242 per month** for tax year **2025**. Sources: same as Q6.

**8.** **No change.** The annual value is ₪2,904 and the monthly value ₪242 in both 2024 and 2025 — the credit point is frozen under the same Economic Efficiency Law cited in answer 4. Sources: same as Q4, Q6.

**9.** **2.25 credit points** — the universal base for every Israeli-resident male. Sources: [wintax](https://wintax.co.il/service/income-tax-credit-points/), [Kolzchut — נקודת זיכוי](https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/%D7%A0%D7%A7%D7%95%D7%93%D7%AA_%D7%96%D7%99%D7%9B%D7%95%D7%99).

**10.** **2.75 credit points** (2.25 resident + 0.5 for being a woman). Same sources as Q9.

## Discharged soldier (§39A)

**11.** **1/6 of a credit point per month** of full regular service for a male who served at least **23 full months** (22 for a female). If regular service was shorter than 23 months, the rate is **1/12 per month** instead. Eligibility requires a minimum of **12 months** of service. The credit only applies against income from personal exertion (יגיעה אישית). Sources: [Kolzchut — נקודות זיכוי לחיילים משוחררים](https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/%D7%A0%D7%A7%D7%95%D7%93%D7%95%D7%AA_%D7%96%D7%99%D7%9B%D7%95%D7%99_%D7%9E%D7%9E%D7%A1_%D7%94%D7%9B%D7%A0%D7%A1%D7%94_%D7%9C%D7%97%D7%99%D7%99%D7%9C%D7%99%D7%9D_%D7%9E%D7%A9%D7%95%D7%97%D7%A8%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9D_%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%A1%D7%99%D7%99%D7%9E%D7%99_%D7%A9%D7%99%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%AA_%D7%9C%D7%90%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%99-%D7%90%D7%96%D7%A8%D7%97%D7%99), [heshbonatun — סעיף 39א](https://www.heshbonatun.co.il/articleView.aspx?aid=280).

**12.** Entitlement begins **the month after the discharge month** and continues for **36 months from the end of the discharge month**. Sources: same as Q11.

**13.** **Tax year 2025** (partial). Per Kolzchut's interpretation: 36 months from end-of-service for a July-2022 discharge runs through **June 2025**, so the last tax year is 2025 covering January–June. Source: [Kolzchut — discharged-soldier credit points](https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/%D7%A0%D7%A7%D7%95%D7%93%D7%95%D7%AA_%D7%96%D7%99%D7%9B%D7%95%D7%99_%D7%9E%D7%9E%D7%A1_%D7%94%D7%9B%D7%A0%D7%A1%D7%94_%D7%9C%D7%97%D7%99%D7%99%D7%9C%D7%99%D7%9D_%D7%9E%D7%A9%D7%95%D7%97%D7%A8%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9D_%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%A1%D7%99%D7%99%D7%9E%D7%99_%D7%A9%D7%99%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%AA_%D7%9C%D7%90%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%99-%D7%90%D7%96%D7%A8%D7%97%D7%99).

> Note: there's a 1-month interpretation gap (36 months from end-July-2022 could land at end-July-2025 if you count month-end-to-month-end, giving 7 eligible months in 2025; Kolzchut's worked example gave June, i.e. 6 months. Either way the **last tax year is 2025**.)

**14.** **2 credit points in tax year 2025.** Reasoning per Kolzchut: 24 months of full regular service ≥ 23 → entitlement is 2 points total (annualised). Discharge December 2024 → eligibility starts January 2025 → tax year 2025 has 12 full eligibility months — soldier receives the full 2-point annual allotment in 2025. Source: [Kolzchut — discharged-soldier credit](https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/%D7%A0%D7%A7%D7%95%D7%93%D7%95%D7%AA_%D7%96%D7%99%D7%9B%D7%95%D7%99_%D7%9E%D7%9E%D7%A1_%D7%94%D7%9B%D7%A0%D7%A1%D7%94_%D7%9C%D7%97%D7%99%D7%99%D7%9C%D7%99%D7%9D_%D7%9E%D7%A9%D7%95%D7%97%D7%A8%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9D_%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%A1%D7%99%D7%99%D7%9E%D7%99_%D7%A9%D7%99%D7%A8%D7%95%D7%AA_%D7%9C%D7%90%D7%95%D7%9E%D7%99-%D7%90%D7%96%D7%A8%D7%97%D7%99).

## §11 settlement benefit

**15.** **7%** for **מדרשת בן גוריון** in tax year 2024. *External sources I located explicitly listed only the 2025 figure* (7%); given the indexation freeze for 2025-2027 (answer 4) and the fact that settlement-list rates carry across years unless the official annex changes them, the 2024 rate is presumed identical at 7%. Source for the 2025 figure (extrapolated to 2024): [taxes-refund — רשימת היישובים 2025](https://taxes-refund.co.il/settlements/).

> ⚠️ One older general-purpose source ([Hilan — הנחה לתושבי ישובים](https://www.hilan.co.il/%D7%9E%D7%A8%D7%9B%D7%96-%D7%99%D7%93%D7%A2/%D7%91%D7%A1%D7%99%D7%A1-%D7%99%D7%93%D7%A2/%D7%9E%D7%90%D7%9E%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%9D/%D7%9E%D7%A1-%D7%94%D7%9B%D7%A0%D7%A1%D7%94-%D7%94%D7%A0%D7%97%D7%94-%D7%9C%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A9%D7%91%D7%99-%D7%99%D7%A9%D7%95%D7%91%D7%99%D7%9D-%D7%9E%D7%A1%D7%95%D7%99%D7%99%D7%9E%D7%99%D7%9D/)) describes a general 15% Negev/Ramat HaNegev rate, which I read as a separate/older benefit category and **not** the §11 settlement rate that applies to מדרשת בן גוריון. Worth manually re-checking the official 2024 ITA settlement annex to confirm.

**16.** **₪146,640/year** for **מדרשת בן גוריון** in tax year **2025**. Source: [taxes-refund — רשימת היישובים 2025](https://taxes-refund.co.il/settlements/).

**17.** **18%** for **לקיה** in tax year **2025**. Source: [taxes-refund](https://taxes-refund.co.il/settlements/) (same table also lists ירוחם and דימונה at 18% / ₪245,400).

**18.** **10%** discount on earned income produced in Eilat or the Hevel Eilot region, up to a ceiling of **₪268,560** (figure stated as applicable for **tax years 2024–2027**). Source: [Kolzchut — זיכוי ממס לתושבי פריפריה](https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/%D7%96%D7%99%D7%9B%D7%95%D7%99_%D7%9E%D7%9E%D7%A1_%D7%94%D7%9B%D7%A0%D7%A1%D7%94_%D7%9C%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A9%D7%91%D7%99%D7%9D_%D7%91%D7%A4%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%A4%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%94).

**19.** **The benefit is pro-rated linearly** by the months of residency in the eligible settlement within the tax year — "חלק היחסי של ההטבה בהתאם למשך מגוריהם ביישוב באותה שנה". For 6/12 months, the discount is half. A separate **12-month consecutive residency** eligibility criterion is also commonly stated, but accountant sources confirm pro-rating applies once eligibility is established. Sources: [Kolzchut — זיכוי לתושבי פריפריה](https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/%D7%96%D7%99%D7%9B%D7%95%D7%99_%D7%9E%D7%9E%D7%A1_%D7%94%D7%9B%D7%A0%D7%A1%D7%94_%D7%9C%D7%AA%D7%95%D7%A9%D7%91%D7%99%D7%9D_%D7%91%D7%A4%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%A4%D7%A8%D7%99%D7%94), [Calcalist — רשימת 400 היישובים](https://www.calcalist.co.il/local/articles/0,7340,L-3624905,00.html).

**20.** **No change** between 2024 and 2025 for מדרשת בן גוריון — rate and ceiling presumed unchanged under the indexation freeze (see Q4). Direct 2024-vs-2025 comparison wasn't surfaced in the sources I fetched, so this is a deduction from the freeze, not a primary source.

## Surtax (מס יסף — §121B)

**27.** **3%** on the portion of annual taxable income above **₪721,560** (equivalent to ₪60,130/month). Combined with the 47% top bracket this yields a 50% effective rate at the top. Sources: [PwC — Israel Individual taxes](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/israel/individual/taxes-on-personal-income), [Protocol](https://en.protocol.co.il/income-tax-rates/), [Y-tax — מס יסף 2025](https://y-tax.co.il/%D7%9E%D7%A1-%D7%A0%D7%95%D7%A1%D7%A3-%D7%A2%D7%9C-%D7%94%D7%9B%D7%A0%D7%A1%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%92%D7%91%D7%95%D7%94%D7%95%D7%AA-%D7%9E%D7%A1-%D7%99%D7%A1%D7%A3-%D7%91%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA-2025/).

**28.** **No change** — the מס יסף threshold and rate are frozen at the 2024 values for 2025-2027 under the Economic Efficiency Law (Answer 4 — the freeze explicitly applies to **§121B brackets**). Sources: [Knesset announcement](https://main.knesset.gov.il/News/PressReleases/pages/press16.12.24p.aspx), [Shibolet — מס יסף 2% נוסף + הקפאת עדכוני המס](https://www.shibolet.com/%D7%94%D7%98%D7%9C%D7%AA-%D7%9E%D7%A1-%D7%99%D7%A1%D7%A3-%D7%A0%D7%95%D7%A1%D7%A3-%D7%91%D7%A9%D7%99%D7%A2%D7%95%D7%A8-%D7%A9%D7%9C-2-%D7%A2%D7%9C-%D7%94%D7%9B%D7%A0%D7%A1%D7%94-%D7%9E%D7%9E%D7%A7/) (note: this also describes a **separate** new 2% surtax on capital-source income, not the §121B threshold change).

## Mandatory filing

**29.** A salaried individual must file an annual return for **tax year 2024** if **either**: (a) **gross income from work or business exceeded ~₪723,000** in the year, **or** (b) **total taxable income from all sources exceeded ₪721,560** (this latter figure triggers the §121B surtax and the §131 mandatory-filing duty in tandem). Source: [Dray & Dray — מי חייב להגיש דוח שנתי](https://cpa-dray.com/he/blog/%D7%9E%D7%99-%D7%97%D7%99%D7%99%D7%91-%D7%9C%D7%94%D7%92%D7%99%D7%A9-%D7%93%D7%95%D7%97-%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%99/).

> Note: my searches surfaced **two adjacent thresholds — ₪723,000 (gross salary) and ₪721,560 (all-source taxable)** — that I had not seen treated separately before. Worth confirming the precise wording in the official תקנות מס הכנסה (פטור מהגשת דין וחשבון) regulation.

**30.** A salaried employee with income from **two or more employers** in 2024 is **required to file** when their combined income exceeds the mandatory-filing thresholds without having obtained תיאום מס. The lack of tax-coordination produces under-withholding because each employer applies the lower brackets and credit points independently as if it were the only employer; the annual return forces a reconciliation. Granular sub-thresholds specific to "two-employer combined" were not explicitly surfaced in the sources I fetched, but the general principle is well-documented. Sources: [Dray & Dray](https://cpa-dray.com/he/blog/%D7%9E%D7%99-%D7%97%D7%99%D7%99%D7%91-%D7%9C%D7%94%D7%92%D7%99%D7%A9-%D7%93%D7%95%D7%97-%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%99/), [Kolzchut — הגשת דו"ח שנתי](https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/%D7%94%D7%92%D7%A9%D7%AA_%D7%93%D7%95%22%D7%97_%D7%A9%D7%A0%D7%AA%D7%99_%D7%9C%D7%9E%D7%A1_%D7%94%D7%9B%D7%A0%D7%A1%D7%94).
