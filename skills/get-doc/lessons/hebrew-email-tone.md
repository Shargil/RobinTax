# Hebrew email tone

When drafting Hebrew correspondence on the user's behalf (council clerks, tax accountants, employer HR, ITA support), write like a regular Israeli, not a lawyer or bot. Israeli clerks expect human-sounding email; AI-typical Hebrew is recognizable and erodes trust.

## Rules

- **No em-dashes (—).** Use comma or period.
- **No legal section citations** in casual asks ("לפי סעיף 11 לפקודת מס הכנסה" is wrong for an email to a clerk). Use plain language: "יישוב מזכה", "החזר מס", "אישור תושב".
- **Attribute retrieved facts to their source.** If a date / address / amount came from a document, write "לפי [המסמך המצורף], …" — don't assert it as personal knowledge of yours or the user's. The user often knows the rough facts, but the specific dates and values came from a document the agent fetched.
- **Don't claim certainty about causes.** If a system rejected the user and the reason isn't proven, write "לא הצלחתי לעבור / לא ברור לי מה הסיבה". Don't invent a cause.
- **Plain greeting "היי"** is fine. "שלום רב" is overkill for a clerk email; reserve for very formal or escalation contexts.
- **Sign off plain**: "תודה רבה, [first name], [phone]". Don't add titles, slogans, or English copy.

## Why it matters

Half the email tax-refund flow is asking small favors from municipal / pension / employer clerks. They reply faster and more helpfully to messages that read like a regular resident wrote them. Over-formal or AI-templated emails get triaged to the bottom or get a "send via the portal" boilerplate response.
