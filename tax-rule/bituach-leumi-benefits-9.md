# §2(2) / §8(ג) / §9 — National-Insurance benefit taxability (מיסוי קצבאות וגמלאות מהמוסד לביטוח לאומי)

## TL;DR
Money the user received from the National Insurance Institute (ביטוח לאומי) splits into two pots for income-tax purposes. **Salary-substitute benefits** — דמי לידה, דמי אבטלה, תגמולי מילואים, דמי פגיעה בעבודה (short-term, ≤91 days), גמלה לשמירת היריון, דמי תאונה — are **taxable as work income** under §2(2); Bituach Leumi withholds tax at source at high (often non-coordinated) rates, and a refund is the rule, not the exception. **Long-term subsistence pensions** — קצבת נכות כללית (§9(5) when ≥75% / 185+ days), קצבת נכות מעבודה (§9(7א)), קצבת ילדים (§9(17)), הבטחת הכנסה, גמלת סיעוד — are **exempt**: not income, not on the return. Edge: §8(ג)(3) lets maternity / unemployment / other short-term benefits straddling year-end be *apportioned* across the two tax years.

## The law
- **Statutes:**
  - Income Tax Ordinance §2(2) (`פקודת מס הכנסה — סעיף 2(2)`) — defines salary, including substitutes paid by a third party (the default-taxable bucket for short-term Bituach Leumi benefits).
  - Income Tax Ordinance §8(ג)(3) (`סעיף 8(ג)(3)`) — apportionment of income earned over a period straddling years (the maternity / unemployment / reserve split-year rule).
  - Income Tax Ordinance §9(5) (`סעיף 9(5)`) — exemption for income of a person with weighted disability ≥75% (or ≥90% by another aggregation), permanent OR ≥185 days. Covers Bituach Leumi נכות כללית.
  - Income Tax Ordinance §9(7א) (`סעיף 9(7א)`) — exemption for compensation from Bituach Leumi for permanent work-injury (קצבת נכות מעבודה).
  - Income Tax Ordinance §9(17) (`סעיף 9(17)`) — exemption for child allowance and other welfare allowances paid by Bituach Leumi.
  - National Insurance Law §350 — list of payers whose recipients are exempt from Bituach Leumi contributions on the pension (orthogonal to income-tax exemption, but commonly conflated by users).
- **Hebrew name:** מיסוי קצבאות וגמלאות המשתלמות מהמוסד לביטוח לאומי.
- **Plain English:** Bituach Leumi pays many kinds of money. Some are wage substitutes (taxable like a paycheck), some are subsistence pensions (exempt). The taxable ones overwithhold by default because Bituach Leumi has no payroll-style coordination with the user's other income — so a year with maternity / unemployment / reserve duty almost always yields a refund. The exempt ones never enter the return at all.

## Eligibility
**Gate (triggers this rule):** any payment received from the National Insurance Institute (ביטוח לאומי) during the tax year, regardless of type.

**Classification table** (what the user got → tax treatment):

| Benefit (Hebrew) | Benefit (English) | Tax treatment | Statute |
|---|---|---|---|
| דמי לידה | Maternity benefits | **Taxable** as work income | §2(2) |
| דמי אבטלה | Unemployment benefits | **Taxable** as work income | §2(2) |
| תגמולי מילואים | Reserve-duty pay | **Taxable** as work income | §2(2) |
| גמלה לשמירת היריון | Pregnancy-preservation allowance | **Taxable** as work income | §2(2) |
| דמי פגיעה בעבודה (≤91 days) | Work-injury short-term | **Taxable** as work income | §2(2) |
| מענק לידה | One-time birth grant | **Exempt** (one-time grant, not income) | §9 family |
| קצבת ילדים | Child allowance | **Exempt** | §9(17) |
| קצבת זיקנה (Bituach Leumi) | Old-age pension (Bituach Leumi) | **Taxable in principle**, but the recipient's age-related credits (§40א + base) typically zero out the liability. For high-income retirees: liability can apply. **NOT covered by §9א** (which is for קצבה מזכה from employer/pension fund only). | §2(5) |
| קצבת שאירים (Bituach Leumi) | Bituach Leumi survivors pension | **Taxable in principle**, same posture as old-age (NOT §9א) | §2(5) |
| קצבת נכות כללית | General disability pension | **Exempt** when the recipient meets §9(5) thresholds (≥75% weighted, OR ≥90% by alt formula, AND permanent OR ≥185 days) | §9(5) |
| קצבת נכות מעבודה (permanent) | Work-injury permanent disability pension | **Exempt** | §9(7א) |
| קצבת שירותים מיוחדים | Special-services pension | **Exempt** | §9 family |
| גמלת ילד נכה | Disabled-child allowance | **Exempt** | §9 family |
| גמלת ניידות | Mobility allowance | **Exempt** | §9 family |
| גמלת סיעוד | Long-term-care allowance | **Exempt** | §9 family |
| הבטחת הכנסה | Income-guarantee allowance | **Exempt** | §9 family |
| קצבת מזונות (Bituach Leumi-collected) | Alimony collected via Bituach Leumi | **Out of scope — pass-through.** Taxability sits with the alimony rule (`alimony` checklist line), not this one. | §9(20) for recipient |
| דמי תאונה (short-term, 90 days) | Accident benefits short-term | **Taxable** as work income | §2(2) |

**For the engine:** only the **Taxable** rows feed `btl_benefits[].taxable_amount` and `withheld_tax`. Exempt rows never enter the input.

## Edge cases
- **§8(ג)(3) split-year apportionment** — when a taxable benefit spans the end of one tax year and the start of the next (the canonical case: maternity leave starting in Q4), the recipient may file **Form 116ג** (`בקשה לפריסת מס הכנסה`) to apportion the income across both years pro-rata by days. Worked example from kolzchut: leave 01.11.2025 → 14.02.2026 (15 weeks = 105 days) → 61 days to 2025, 44 days to 2026. Drives a refund when the off-year had less income (different bracket). Applies to maternity, unemployment, reserve, work-injury (short-term), pregnancy-preservation — i.e. every Taxable row.
- **6-year retroactive claim** — like every annual-return refund, claimable up to 6 tax years back. Aligned with the general ITA refund window, NOT a Bituach Leumi-specific rule. (The §8(ג)(3) apportionment itself must be elected on the relevant year's return.)
- **Bituach Leumi withholding is per-payment, not coordinated** — Bituach Leumi withholds at source on every taxable benefit it pays, but it has no payroll-style תיאום מס with the user's other employers. Most users with a part-year benefit + part-year salary end up over-withheld → refund. This is one of the highest-yield refund drivers in the entire system, especially for women on maternity leave.
- **§9(5) gate is not the Bituach Leumi disability percentage** — §9(5) requires a determination by the *income-tax medical board* (ועדה רפואית למס הכנסה), not the Bituach Leumi disability percentage. The Bituach Leumi percentage may differ; users routinely confuse the two. The exemption applies to income tax on **all income** during the qualifying period, not just on the Bituach Leumi pension itself.
- **§9(5) "permanent OR ≥185 days"** — the 185-day rule allows temporary high-disability periods to qualify. Below-threshold or below-185 days → §9(5) does NOT apply; the pension remains exempt only if §9(7א) (work-injury) or §9(17) (welfare allowance) applies independently.
- **Old-age pension (קצבת זיקנה) is not §9א-exempt** — §9א applies to קצבה מזכה paid by an employer or קופת גמל לקצבה. Bituach Leumi's old-age pension is statutorily distinct. In practice, retirees' pensions usually fall under the bracket-and-credit threshold (base resident points + §40א age credits zero out liability), so the question "is Bituach Leumi old-age pension taxed?" is academic for most users. **For high-income retirees with material pension fund income on top, liability can apply.**
- **קצבת מזונות (alimony via Bituach Leumi) is a pass-through** — Bituach Leumi collects alimony from the obligated parent and pays it to the custodial parent; the tax treatment belongs to the alimony rule (`alimony` checklist line, §9(20) for recipient when court-ordered, §17(7) deduction for payer). Do not double-count if the user lists Bituach Leumi as the source.
- **Stacking** — taxable Bituach Leumi benefits stack with salary in the same bracket pass. The engine adds them to `grossOrdinary` and treats them identically to wage income. Credit points are applied once over the aggregate, not per source.
- **No effect on credit points (נקודות זיכוי)** — receiving a Bituach Leumi benefit does not grant or revoke credit points. The base resident points remain; soldier / degree / immigrant points remain whatever they would have been.
- **Children of 18+ trigger benefit termination** — קצבת ילדים stops automatically; not a tax event.

## Formula
The engine has no Bituach Leumi-benefit-specific helper. Taxable Bituach Leumi benefits flow through the default Calculator pipeline identically to wage income:

```
gross_ordinary   = sum(emp.taxable_income for emp in employments)
                 + sum(btl.taxable_amount for btl in btl_benefits)   # ONLY taxable rows

total_withheld   = sum(emp.withheld_tax   for emp in employments)
                 + sum(btl.withheld_tax   for btl in btl_benefits)   # withholding flows symmetrically
                 + investment_income.separate_withheld_tax

# Default pipeline runs unchanged from here.
ordinary_tax     = applyBrackets(gross_ordinary - §47, brackets)
                   - credit_amount - settlement_discount - donation_credit - pension_45a_credit
                   # clamped at 0
refund_or_owe    = total_withheld - (ordinary_tax + separate_tax)
```

The mechanic this rule encodes is **the classifier** (which Bituach Leumi line items become `btl_benefits[]` entries vs. which are dropped because exempt), plus the §8(ג)(3) apportionment edge case which is implemented at the *intake* layer (when the user reports a year-spanning benefit, intake asks whether to apportion → that re-shapes the per-year `taxable_amount`s). The engine itself receives only post-classification numbers.

## Intake
Read by the `intake` skill walker when the user checks **either** `btl_income` **or** `maternity_received` in [`Intake/checklist.md`](../Intake/checklist.md). Both checklist lines route to this single spec — `maternity_received` is intentionally retained as a duplicate quick-path for the most common refund driver (we're not building a separate maternity-only rule). The walker de-duplicates the follow-up queue when both are checked. Design philosophy: **fetch the `bituach-leumi-payments` confirmation and let the document enumerate per-benefit amounts + withholding. Only ask follow-ups that the document itself can't answer.** That keeps intake minimal.

### Gate
- **Checklist labels (both route here):**
  - `btl_income` — "קיבלתי כסף מביטוח לאומי (דמי אבטלה, דמי לידה, קצבת זיקנה וכו')"
  - `maternity_received` — "קיבלתי דמי לידה"
- On `yes` (either) → ask the follow-ups below.
- On `no` (both) → no follow-ups. Engine receives `btl_benefits: []`.

### Follow-ups

- **`btl_categories`** — Description (`rule_lede`, plain Hebrew, shown above the question): "חלק מהתשלומים מביטוח לאומי (כמו דמי לידה ודמי אבטלה) הם הכנסה חייבת במס, וביטוח לאומי ניכה מהם מס במקור בלי לתאם עם המשכורת שלך, לכן לפעמים נוכה יותר מדי מס, ויוצא החזר." `AskUserQuestion` multi-select, ordered by refund-frequency (most common first):
  1. `Maternity / pregnancy preservation (דמי לידה / שמירת היריון)`
  2. `Unemployment (דמי אבטלה)`
  3. `A long-term pension — old-age / survivors / disability (קצבת זיקנה / שאירים / נכות)`
  4. `Other short-term benefit — work injury / accident (דמי פגיעה / דמי תאונה)`

  Reason: sets expectations for which lines to read off the `bituach-leumi-payments` doc; the doc (not this answer) is the source of truth for amounts + withholding. Maps to profile-only flag. **Conditional follow-up:** if `long-term pension` is checked, queue `btl_pension_type` next.

- **`btl_pension_type`** — **Conditional**: only if `btl_categories` includes long-term pension. `AskUserQuestion` multi-select (4 chips):
  1. `Old-age (קצבת זיקנה)`
  2. `Survivors (קצבת שאירים)`
  3. `General disability (קצבת נכות כללית)`
  4. `Work-injury permanent (קצבת נכות מעבודה)`

  Reason: long-term pension is the one category where the document may not clearly route to taxable vs exempt — old-age/survivors are taxable in principle, disability types are typically exempt (`קצבת נכות מעבודה` via §9(7א) automatically; `קצבת נכות כללית` only with an income-tax medical board decision — next follow-up). **Conditional follow-up:** if `General disability` is checked, queue `tax_medical_board_decision`.

- **`tax_medical_board_decision`** — **Conditional**: only if `btl_pension_type` includes `General disability`. `AskUserQuestion` chips:
  1. `Yes — I have an exemption decision from the income-tax medical board (ועדה רפואית לפטור ממס הכנסה)`
  2. `No / never applied`
  3. `I don't know`

  Reason: Bituach Leumi's own disability percentage is NOT the gate. A *separate* income-tax medical board (ועדה רפואית לפטור ממס הכנסה) must issue an exemption decision; without it, the pension is treated as taxable income (Bituach Leumi withholds, refund mechanic applies). On `I don't know` → seed the decision letter as a manual §11 HANDOFF step (no `get-doc` playbook yet).

### Things we intentionally do NOT ask at intake

Documenting the no's so a future contributor doesn't re-add them.

- **Reserve duty pay (תגמולי מילואים)** — for salaried users, reserve income is paid *by the employer* (Bituach Leumi reimburses behind the scenes). It appears on Form 106, not on a Bituach Leumi confirmation. The multi-employer rule [`tax-rule/multi-employer-121-164.md`](multi-employer-121-164.md) catches any over-withholding. Only self-employed reservists (out of v1 scope) would see direct Bituach Leumi payments. **Removed from option list.**
- **Which exempt benefits the user received** (קצבת ילדים, גמלת סיעוד, הבטחת הכנסה, גמלת ילד נכה, etc.) — these never appear on the Bituach Leumi tax-confirmation doc because they're not taxable. Asking is pure noise; nothing the answer would change feeds the engine.
- **Whether the user has the amounts** — we ALWAYS need the official confirmation doc anyway (for citable source + per-benefit breakdown + withheld tax). No path skips the fetch.
- **Whether the benefit straddled Dec/Jan (§8(ג)(3) apportionment)** — the *dates* needed to detect a straddle live on the doc, not in the user's head. Surfaces *post-doc* when the Calculator sees a Dec/Jan boundary on a taxable line, not at intake. See `Open questions`.
- **Whether the retiree has high other income (high-income old-age case)** — same reason: derive from the union of Form 106 + the Bituach Leumi doc post-collection, not from the user.

- On `tax_medical_board_decision = I don't know` → surface as manual step in §11 HANDOFF (fetch the ועדה רפואית לפטור ממס הכנסה decision letter); not yet a `get-doc` playbook.

## Open questions
- **§9(5) eligibility determination** — getting the ועדה רפואית לפטור ממס הכנסה decision letter is a manual, often-multi-month process. No `get-doc` playbook today. Branch surfaces as §11 HANDOFF when fired.
- **Post-doc §8(ג)(3) apportionment detection** — Calculator should detect a Dec/Jan straddle on any taxable Bituach Leumi line and surface the Form 116ג election as an optional refund booster. Not built; tracked here so it doesn't get lost. The intake side intentionally does *not* ask the user — dates live on the doc.
- **Form 116ג apportionment fetch / fill** — not built. Surfaces when the post-doc detector fires.
- **Bituach Leumi old-age pension high-income case** — engine treats any taxable Bituach Leumi income identically to salary income. We do not currently auto-detect "user is a high-income retiree who needs to declare Bituach Leumi old-age as income" because v1 user base is salaried wage-earners, not retirees. This is structurally handled (just put the amount in `btl_benefits[].taxable_amount`) but UX-wise the follow-ups only flag the case, they don't drive a fetch.
- **§9א coupling check** — there is no live tax-rule spec for §9א (קצבה מזכה — private pension exemption). When that rule is canonicalized, the `pension_high_income_check` follow-up here may want to cross-link, since high-income retirees often have BOTH Bituach Leumi old-age pension AND קצבה מזכה.
