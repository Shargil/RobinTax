# Tax Refund — Processing Time, Interest & Amounts

Shared mechanics that apply once a refund request (135) or annual return (1301) has been submitted. Covers how long it takes, what indexation/interest the refund accrues, and what typical refund amounts look like. Does NOT cover *how to file* — see [tax-refund-salaried.md](tax-refund-salaried.md) and [tax-refund-self-employed.md](tax-refund-self-employed.md).

## Processing time

- **Practical**: typical clean submissions are processed in **90–180 business days** (~4.5–9 months).
- **Statutory ceiling**: the refund must be issued within **1 year of the שומה being issued**, or **2 years from the end of the tax year** in which the tax was paid, whichever is **later**.
- **What slows it down**: missing documents, mismatch between the form and the 106/1320 data, ITA queries (דרישת הבהרה), prior open assessments, an outstanding tax debt offsetting the refund.

## Linkage and interest

- Refund amounts are linked to the **CPI (מדד)** and bear **4% annual interest**.
- **First two years carry no interest and no linkage.** Interest + linkage start accruing only from the **third year** after the tax year in question (i.e., from January following the second year after the relevant tax year).
- This matters for old claims — a refund for tax year 2020 filed in late 2026 has been earning index + 4% for the past ~4 years, which can add meaningfully to the principal.

## Typical refund amounts

- Industry-reported averages cite **~10,500 ₪** for claims that succeed via tax-refund services. Treat this as a marketing benchmark, **not** a promise: it is biased by long lookback periods, multi-year bundles, and self-selection (people who try are those who suspect a refund exists).
- A "no-refund" outcome is common and legitimate — many filers will get 0 ₪ or even discover a debt.

## How the refund arrives

- Wired by bank transfer to the account whose **אישור ניהול חשבון** was uploaded with the filing.
- ITA mails a **שומה** notice (assessment) showing income figures and tax calc. Keep this notice — it's the official record of what was paid back and why.
- If the filer has any open ITA debt (income tax, מע״מ, ניכויים), it is **offset against the refund** before the balance is transferred.

## Gotchas

- **No interest in years 1–2** surprises filers — a 1,000 ₪ refund processed 8 months after filing is still exactly 1,000 ₪.
- **Old claims compound** — by year 3+ the linkage + 4% become non-trivial; this is why retroactive 6-year filings can dwarf single-year filings.
- **Refund offset** — a forgotten ביטוח לאומי or מע״מ debt can silently consume the refund. Check open balances before celebrating.
- **The clock on the statutory deadline starts at "שומה issued", not at filing.** ITA can delay issuing שומה for legitimate reasons.
